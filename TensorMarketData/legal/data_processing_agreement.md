# Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

**Last Updated: February 12, 2026**

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into between **TensorMarketData, Inc.** ("Processor") and the enterprise customer signing the relevant order form or master agreement ("Controller"). This DPA supplements the main Terms of Service or Enterprise Agreement.

## 1. Definitions

- **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person as defined under GDPR, CCPA, or applicable law.
- **"Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, storage, use, transfer, or deletion.
- **"Sub-processor"** means third parties we engage to process Personal Data on your behalf.
- **"Services"** means the TensorMarketData platform, APIs, and related services.

## 2. Scope and Purpose

This DPA applies when TensorMarketData processes Personal Data on behalf of Controller in providing the Services. The purpose of processing is to:
- Provide the data marketplace platform
- Enable data transactions between providers and buyers
- Support API integrations and agent functionality
- Maintain and improve Services

## 3. Roles and Obligations

### Controller (You)
- Determine the purposes and means of processing
- Ensure you have legal basis to share data with us
- Ensure Personal Data is accurate and limited to what's necessary
- Respond to data subject requests within required timeframes

### Processor (Us)
- Process Personal Data only on documented instructions
- Ensure personnel are confidentiality obligated
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- Assist Controller with data subject rights requests
- Delete or return data upon termination

## 4. Personal Data We Process

| Data Category | Examples | Purpose |
|---------------|----------|---------|
| Account data | Name, email, company, role | Service provision, communication |
| Transaction data | Purchases, licenses, usage | Order fulfillment, billing |
| Technical data | IP address, device info, logs | Security, troubleshooting |
| Content data | Data listings, queries, agent interactions | Service functionality |

## 5. Security Measures

We implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures:

### Technical Measures
- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Encryption at rest for sensitive data
- Multi-factor authentication for administrative access
- Role-based access controls
- Intrusion detection and logging
- Regular vulnerability scanning

### Organizational Measures
- Data Protection Officer appointed
- Employee confidentiality agreements
- Security training programs
- Incident response procedures
- Vendor due diligence process

## 6. Sub-Processors

We may engage Sub-processors for hosting, payment processing, analytics, and support tools. Current Sub-processors listed in Appendix A.

We will:
- Ensure Sub-processor contracts include equivalent data protection obligations
- Notify you of any new Sub-processors
- Give you opportunity to object (30 days to object, otherwise deemed accepted)

You authorize us to engage Sub-processors generally under these terms.

## 7. Data Subject Rights

We will assist Controller in responding to data subject requests for:

| Right | Our Assistance |
|-------|-----------------|
| Access | Provide system capabilities to retrieve data |
| Rectification | Enable data updates through account settings |
| Erasure | Delete data upon request (where feasible) |
| Portability | Export data in machine-readable format |
| Objection | Disable processing for specific purposes |

Response time: We will assist within **10 business days** of request.

## 8. Data Transfers

### International Transfers
Personal Data may be transferred outside the origin country, including to the United States.

**Safeguards for EU/EEA Transfers:**
- EU-US Data Privacy Framework certification (if applicable)
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by EU Commission
- Transfer Impact Assessments conducted

### UK Transfers
UK Extension to SCCs applies for transfers from the United Kingdom.

## 9. Security Incidents

### Incident Response
1. We will notify Controller within **72 hours** of discovering a Personal Data breach
2. Notification will include nature of breach, categories and approximate number of data subjects affected, likely consequences, and mitigation measures
3. We will provide ongoing updates as information becomes available

### Incident Management
- We will investigate the incident
- Take remedial action
- Cooperate with regulatory authorities as required

## 10. Audits and Compliance

### Audit Rights
Controller may audit our compliance with this DPA:
- Annual self-assessment report available upon request
- SOC 2 Type II report available under NDA
- On-site audit: 30 days advance notice, at Controller's expense, mutually convenient time

### Certifications
We maintain:
- SOC 2 Type II
- ISO 27001 certification in progress

## 11. Data Return and Deletion

### Upon Termination
- Controller may export data using built-in export features for 30 days post-termination
- After 30 days, we will delete all Personal Data within 90 days unless legally required to retain
- Deletion confirmation provided upon request

### Exceptions
We may retain data as required by law (e.g., tax records) or for legal claims. Such retention is limited to what's legally required.

## 12. Liability

Liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations in the main Terms of Service. Aggregate liability for data protection claims shall not exceed **$500,000** or the fees paid in the 12 months preceding the claim, whichever is greater.

## 13. Term and Termination

- This DPA runs for the duration of the main service agreement
- Termination of the service agreement terminates this DPA
- Termination does not relieve obligations regarding data return and deletion

## 14. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the laws of Delaware, USA. Disputes resolved in Delaware courts.
